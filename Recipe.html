How to make a sweet and spiced Somali tea

Somali tea is very special. Although everyone uses similar ingredients, Somali tea tastes different in every home. See below for the ingredients and steps needed to make this sweet and delicious tea.

Ingredients

  • 6 cups
  • 2 whole cloves
  • 4 green cardamom pods
  • One (3- to 4-inch) cinnamon stick
  • 3 1/4 ounces unpeeled fresh ginger, or to taste
  • 1/3 cup (67 grams) granulated sugar, or to taste
  • 1 tablespoon loose leaf black tea or 2 tea bags
  • Powdered milk, such as Nestle Nido brand, or fresh milk of choice (optional)

Steps to making the tea.

  1. In a medium pot over medium-high heat, bring the water to a boil. While waiting for the water to boil, in a mortar with a pestle, pound the cloves, cardamom and cinnamon until roughly ground. Alternatively, place the spices in a spice grinder and process until roughly ground. Add the ground spices to the water.
  2. Slice the ginger root into 1/4-inch thick pieces. Working with a few pieces at a time, smash the ginger in a mortar and pestle until all pieces are smashed (you can also smash with a meat tenderizer or a kitchen mallet). Add the ginger to the water, followed by the sugar and tea, and bring to a boil.
  3. Once the mixture comes to a boil, turn off the heat and let the tea steep on the burner for 10 to 15 minutes. Strain into cups and serve with milk of your choice, if desired.
